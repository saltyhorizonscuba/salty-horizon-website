The 10 Best Beaches Near Tamarindo, Costa Rica

Tamarindo sits in the middle of one of Guanacaste's best stretches of coast, and every beach around it has its own personality. Here's the practical rundown — distances, who each one suits, and the one thing to know before you go.

Salty HorizonJuly 21, 20266 min read

Tamarindo isn't short on beaches, but it's easy to burn a whole day picking the wrong one for what you actually want to do. Some of these are built for a full day of swimming with the kids, some are surf-only, and a couple are barely developed at all. Here are ten worth knowing about, all reachable by car, with what actually matters before you pack up and go.

1. Playa Conchal

  • 18 km · ~30 min from Tamarindo
  • Best for families, swimmers, snorkeling
  • Bring proper sandals — patches of shoreline are rockier than they look

Conchal's sand is famously made of crushed shells and coral fragments rather than typical grains, which gives it a coarse, slightly sparkling texture underfoot. The water clarity here is some of the best on this stretch of coast, and it stays calm enough for young swimmers most of the year — a real draw for snorkeling, since you can often see straight to the bottom in the shallows without even a mask. Go early if you can: by midday it's the most visited beach on this list, and the walk down from the parking area gets busy.

2. Playa Avellanas

  • ~30 min from Tamarindo
  • Best for intermediate–advanced surfers
  • Weekend parking fills up fast — arrive early

Laid-back and more spread out than Tamarindo itself, Avellanas has a handful of solid beachfront sodas for lunch and a noticeably quieter vibe overall. It's a favorite for intermediate-to-advanced surfers thanks to its consistent beach break — just get there early on weekends, parking fills up fast. Just inland, a wooden boardwalk winds through the mangrove forest that backs the beach; it's an easy, shaded detour from the sun and a good spot to see the estuary birdlife up close.

3. Playa Grande

  • ~25 min from Tamarindo
  • Best for long walks, turtle nesting season
  • Night access restricted in nesting season — check tide times first

A wide, wild stretch of sand backed by Las Baulas National Marine Park, and one of Costa Rica's key leatherback turtle nesting sites in season. It's less developed than the beaches closer to town, which is part of the appeal if what you want is a long, quiet walk rather than a beach club. Check tide times and nighttime access rules before you go — the beach is restricted after dark during nesting season specifically to protect the turtles, and it's enforced.

4. Playa Langosta

  • A few minutes south of Tamarindo
  • Best for sunsets, easy strolls
  • Rockier underfoot — check conditions before swimming

Just south of Tamarindo and rockier underfoot than most on this list, but one of the best spots in town to watch the sun go down — the horizon here is wide open with almost nothing blocking it. Swimming conditions shift by section and by tide, so it's worth checking locally before wading in. It's more of an easy add-on to an evening in Tamarindo than a destination beach on its own.

5. Playa Penca

  • ~40 min from Tamarindo
  • Best for calm, clear-water swimming
  • Little natural shade — bring water & sunscreen

Secluded and clear, and genuinely good for a quiet swim without much of a crowd — this is the one to pick if the plan is simply to float in calm water for a few hours. There's very little natural shade along the sand, so bring more water and sunscreen than you think you'll need, and get there earlier in the day before the sun gets high.

6. Playa Prieta

  • Past Playa Grande
  • Best for a quieter, local scene
  • Access road is rough in spots

A small, peaceful cove with a few shaded patches and a more local feel than the beaches closer to town — you're far more likely to share it with a Tico family than with other tourists. The access road can be a little rough in a regular car, but the quiet usually makes it worth the drive.

7. Playa Minas

  • Near Playa Prieta
  • Best for off-the-beaten-path travelers
  • Access and currents can be unpredictable — swim with caution

Wilder and far less visited than anywhere else on this list — the pick if what you actually want is a beach with nobody on it. Access can be tricky, especially after rain, and conditions are less predictable than the more sheltered beaches further south, so swim with caution here and don't expect any services.

8. Playa Brasilito

  • Up the coast, near Flamingo
  • Best for local fishing-village feel
  • Good spot to pair beach time with a meal

A long beach fronting an active fishing village, with a genuinely local, unpolished feel — you'll see fishing boats pulled up on the sand rather than sunbeds. It's an easy one to pair with a meal right on the beach, and a good stop if you want a slower, more authentic afternoon than the more built-up beaches nearby.

9. Playa Potrero

  • Next to Brasilito
  • Best for calm bays, boat tours
  • Common departure point for coastal boat trips

Calm bays and an unhurried pace next door to Brasilito, with water that stays sheltered even when the rest of the coast picks up wind. This stretch is a common starting point for boat tours, if seeing the coastline from the water appeals more than the beach itself.

10. Playa Flamingo

  • ~30 min from Tamarindo
  • Best for easy access, boat trips, sunsets
  • The most developed beach on this list

Light-colored sand and one of the more developed, easy-access beaches on this list, with a marina nearby and more restaurants within walking distance than anywhere else here. It's a reliable choice if a boat trip or a sunset with a drink in hand is the main plan for the day.

Which one should you actually pick?

Every beach on this list has its own personality, and none of them is really the wrong choice — it depends what you're there for that day. If what you're actually chasing is what's under the water rather than next to it, the Catalina Islands are the call: the boat rides out to them leave from this same stretch of coast, a short trip away from most of the beaches above.

Frequently asked questions

Which beach near Tamarindo is best for swimming?

Playa Conchal and Playa Penca both have calm, clear water. Conchal is the easier drive at about 30 minutes; Penca is quieter but further out, around 40 minutes.

Where can I see sea turtles nesting near Tamarindo?

Playa Grande, inside the protected Las Baulas National Marine Park, is one of Costa Rica's key leatherback turtle nesting beaches. Nesting season restricts night access to the beach, so check current rules before visiting.

Which beach near Tamarindo is best for surfing?

Playa Avellanas and Playa Grande are the two most commonly recommended for intermediate-to-advanced surfers on this stretch of coast.

What is the closest beach to Tamarindo?

Playa Langosta, just a few minutes south of town.

Is Tamarindo a good base for scuba diving as well as beaches?

Yes. The Catalina Islands, one of the region's main dive sites, are a short boat ride from this same coastline, which is why most dive charters, including ours, depart right from Tamarindo.
